<p><p>The lithium-ion battery recycling technology market is divided into notable types, particularly cascade utilization and conventional recycling. Cascade utilization focuses on reusing batteries for secondary applications, extending their life before recycling. This method maximizes resource efficiency and minimizes waste. In contrast, conventional recycling involves recovering valuable materials, such as lithium, cobalt, and nickel, through processes like pyrometallurgy or hydrometallurgy. Both approaches aim to reduce environmental impact and improve sustainability within the growing electric vehicle and renewable energy sectors.</p></p>
